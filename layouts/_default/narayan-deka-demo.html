The Starting Point

Why 2021 happened ‚Äî and why it won't happen again

In 2021, BJP won Assam. Narayan Deka lost Barkhetri. That single fact is the most important insight in this entire campaign ‚Äî because it means the loss was personal, not political.

A 12,000+ vote swing against him in a BJP wave year points to one thing: perceived absence. Voters felt their MLA wasn't present, wasn't delivering, wasn't theirs. Against the Barman dynasty ‚Äî a former Chief Minister's family with three consecutive wins ‚Äî that absence is fatal.

The campaign doesn't paper over this. It confronts it directly ‚Äî and answers it with the one thing no rally speech can manufacture: demonstrated, tangible action at every doorstep in the constituency.

The verdict of 2021 was not anti-BJP. It was anti-absence. This campaign's entire architecture is built to answer that verdict ‚Äî village by village, household by household.

The Three-Part Answer
01
The Scheme Portal ‚Äî "Aapunar Adhikar"
A constituency-wide scheme eligibility service. 12 questions. Every household in all 124 villages profiled. Every family gets a printed slip listing what they're entitled to ‚Äî in Assamese. This is the campaign's ground infrastructure and its most powerful trust signal.
02
The Adhikar Mitra Network
60 trained local volunteers ‚Äî one per village cluster ‚Äî who run camps, follow up on cases, and track benefit delivery. On polling day, this network becomes the turnout machine. Every volunteer is a trusted face in their own village. You can't buy that.
03
The Accountability Narrative
Narayan Deka addresses 2021 directly ‚Äî not defensively. A 3-minute video in Assamese, sitting, no podium: "You told me I fell short. I listened." Then the numbers speak: households reached, schemes matched, Orunodoi enrollments completed. Action over words.

Communications

Four channels. One constituency. Zero wasted messages.

In a 100% rural, 48% literacy constituency, mass media doesn't move votes. The tea stall does. The trusted neighbour does. WhatsApp voice notes in Assamese do. Every communications decision flows from this reality.
